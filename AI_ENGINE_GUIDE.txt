strategies, and format protocols for artificial intelligence agents participating

AI models (specifically Claude Fable-5, Claude Opus 4.8, and GPT-5.5 Pro) are

1. PURPOSE OF THE CHALLENGE
---------------------------
The objective of this challenge is to evaluate and compare the spatial reasoning,
resource tracking, and structural planning capabilities of advanced language
models in a simulated 3D voxel sandbox environment.

As an AI agent, you are competing directly against other models to build the most
structurally sound, complete, and aesthetically satisfying shelter.

To qualify as a valid "Complete Shelter", your design MUST have:
* Four (4) cohesive walls sealing the perimeter.
* A solid overhead roof covering the interior space.
* Exactly one (1) vertical door opening measuring 1 block wide by 2 blocks high.
* A stable structure built cleanly upon the terrain.

Every block counts. Efficiency, layout planning, and resource conservation will
be heavily weighed by the automatic grading program and human judges.


2. AVAILABLE INFORMATION
------------------------
At the start of every turn, your agent will receive a standardized state payload
containing the following structured data fields:

* Current Position:
  The absolute coordinates of your player/camera inside the 3D world, rounded to
  nearest integer, formatted as {x, y, z}. Custom blocks should be placed on or
  around these coordinates.

* Inventory:
  A key-value state mapping with counts of your current available block resources.
  Example: {"5": 64, "4": 48, ...} where the keys are Block IDs and values are
  the quantity of blocks remaining in your inventory.

* Surroundings Grid:
  A 2D horizontal slice of block IDs surrounding your current standing plane. The
  grid is of size N x N (dependent on your active model handicap). Your standing
  coordinate is always located precisely at the center coordinate of this grid. This
  allows you to map nearby terrain, existing walls, and obstacles.

* Current Turn Number:
  Your active turn progress indicator. Your budget is constrained between Turn 1
  and Turn 15.

* Block ID Reference:
  A list of available voxel indexes in the active sandbox engine.

4. TURN SYSTEM SPECIFICATIONS
-----------------------------
* You have a total budget of 15 turns. 
* On each turn, you can output at most thirty (30) block placements or deletions.
* Placing more than 30 blocks in a single turn will result in the execution engine
  truncating your response and accepting only the first 30 valid items.
* You must plan and design ahead. Treat the 15-turn limit as a tight constraint. Do
  not attempt to build the entire structure on Turn 1; distribute the load.


5. RESPONSE FORMAT MANDATE
--------------------------
To successfully communicate blocks to the execution engine, you MUST respond
with ONLY a raw, valid JSON array containing coordinate objects.

An object in the array represents a single block action and must contain:
"x" -> absolute X-coordinate integer
"y" -> absolute Y-coordinate (height) integer
"z" -> absolute Z-coordinate integer
"id" -> target block ID (0-12)

CORRECT FORMAT EXAMPLE (RAW JSON):
[
  {"x": 0, "y": 14, "z": 0, "id": 5},
  {"x": 1, "y": 14, "z": 0, "id": 5},
  {"x": 2, "y": 14, "z": 0, "id": 5}
]

CRITICAL WRONG FORMAT VIOLATIONS (WILL DEFEAT PARSERS AND VOID THE TURN):
* Do not wrap the JSON output in markdown formatting (e.g. no triple backticks like ```json ... ```).
* Do not include any preambles, explanations, remarks, postscripts, or commentary before or after the JSON.
* Do not write conversational prose ("Sure, here are your blocks:", etc.).
* Do NOT provide empty space, lists, or multiple json blocks.

Failing to write valid JSON or exposing verbose conversation text will result in a
Parser Crash, which immediately voids that turn's placements (Turn advances, but 0
blocks are placed).


6. RECOMMENDED BUILDING STRATEGY
---------------------------------
Follow this logical phase breakdown to build a perfect house in 15 turns:

Optional: MOAT / WATER DEFENSE (around the house)
- Dig a 1-block-wide trench ring using ID 0 (Air) around your shelter perimeter.
- Fill the trench by placing ID 7 (Water) into the dug coordinates.
- This creates a simple water moat that looks great and adds a clean perimeter.

Optional: FARMING / DECORATION (like the referenced farm images)
- Use the Shovel Tool (ID 33) to create walking paths (it converts to Path ID 36).
- Build farm rows using Farmland (ID 40).
- Place crops on top of farmland: Wheat (ID 37) / Carrot (ID 38).
- Place Sugarcane (ID 39) next to water for a realistic look.

Turn 1: Foundation Phase
- Analyze the coordinates of the surrounding terrain grid.
- Choose a flat open coordinate set.
- Lay down a rectangular perimeter foundation (e.g., 5x5 or 6x6) on the floor.

Turns 2 to 5: Base Walls Phase
- Set Wood (5) or Stone (4) blocks along your foundation perimeter.
- Outline where your door opening will be located on one wall.

Turns 6 to 10: Height Expansion Phase
- Stack blocks up to a minimum clearance height of 3 blocks high.
- Verify that your wall heights are uniform to support a flat or sloped roof.

Turns 11 to 13: Roof Phase
- Place a flat layer or stairs of Wood (5), Stone (4), or Leaves (6) on top of the
  walls (Y-level +3 above ground).
- Seal off the interior completely from above.

Turns 14 to 15: Finishes & Audits Phase
- Double check that your door opening is exactly 1 block wide and 2 blocks high.
- Correct any accidental double placements or gaps using ID 0 (Air) to clear.
- Add decorative blocks or architectural highlights.


7. COORDINATE SYSTEM EXPLAINED
------------------------------
The game uses a standard 3D cartesian coordinate system. All your placements must
reference absolute coordinates.

* X-Axis (Horizontal North/South):
  - Increasing X (positive) moves NORTH.
  - Decreasing X (negative) moves SOUTH.

* Z-Axis (Horizontal East/West):
  - Increasing Z (positive) moves EAST.
  - Decreasing Z (negative) moves WEST.

* Y-Axis (Vertical Altitude):
  - Increasing Y (positive) moves UP.
  - Decreasing Y (negative) moves DOWN.

Coord Visual Chart:
                   +Y (Up)
                     |
                     |
  -Z (West) <--------0--------> +Z (East)  (Standing Plane)
                    / \
                   /   \
         -X (South)     +X (North)

Example: If your standing position is {x: 0, y: 14, z: 0}, placing a block directly
on your head would be {x: 0, y: 16, z: 0}. Placing a block right in front of your feet
would be {x: 1, y: 14, z: 0}.

10. WINNING CRITERIA
--------------------
Structures are evaluated by automated test sequences and human evaluators on:
1. Completeness: Are all 4 walls, roof, and door opening fully represented?
2. Structural Integrity: Are there floating or disconnected blocks in the air?
3. Efficiency: Was block waste kept to a minimum? Did you finish under budget?
4. Cohesion: Is the choice of materials balanced and uniform?
5. Appearance: Does the final build look like a structured, clean home design?


11. RECAP OF IMPORTANT RULES
----------------------------
* NEVER write conversational text or markdown code guards. Only output raw JSON.
* Do not place more than 30 blocks in a single turn.
* Build on flat, stable ground. Do not start walls in the middle of lakes or air.
* Maintain a door clearance of exactly 1x2 to avoid penalty adjustments.
* Budget your turn actions carefully. Design and map the house before taking action.

16. THINK BIG - BUILD COMPLETE STRUCTURES

---

Do not immediately optimize for tiny houses.

A tiny 3x3 shelter may technically satisfy minimum requirements, but it rarely wins on appearance, creativity, or architectural quality.

Think beyond survival boxes.

Aim to create structures that feel intentional and impressive:

* Large houses
* Cabins
* Towers
* Courtyards
* Gardens
* Shops
* Watchtowers
* Small castles
* Multi-room buildings

Recommended Building Process:

Phase 1 (Turns 1-5): Prototype & Structure Planning

* Establish the full footprint of the build.
* Mark major walls, entrances, corners, and roof supports.
* Decide the overall architectural style.
* Create the skeleton of the structure before focusing on details.

Phase 2 (Turns 6-10): Core Construction

* Complete walls.
* Add height variation.
* Build roofs and structural supports.
* Ensure the structure is visually balanced from multiple angles.

Phase 3 (Turns 11-15): Decoration & Refinement

* Add windows using glass.
* Install doors and entrances.
* Add fences, gates, gardens, paths, and lighting.
* Improve symmetry and aesthetics.
* Add decorative interior and exterior elements.

Important Principle:

A beautiful unfinished large structure is often more impressive than a perfectly completed tiny box.

Think like an architect, not a block placer.

Create something that another player would want to explore, not merely something that satisfies the minimum requirements.
